Prabhas

Prabhas is a renowned Indian actor, particularly famous for his work in Telugu cinema. He rose to international fame with his portrayal of Amarendra Baahubali in the Baahubali series, which made him a household name across India and globally. Born on October 23, 1979, in Chennai, Prabhas comes from a film-oriented family and has had a passion for acting from an early age. His breakthrough film was Varsham (2004), which established him as a star in Telugu cinema. Prabhas is known for his dedication to roles, often undergoing extreme physical transformations for his characters. His versatility can be seen in films like Radhe Shyam and Saaho, where he moved between different genres. His fan following is massive, especially for his larger-than-life roles. Beyond acting, Prabhas is known for his humility and down-to-earth nature, which has earned him the title of "Pan-Indian Star." His massive success in both Telugu and Hindi films has made him one of the biggest stars in India, with a reputation for professionalism and sincerity.

NTR

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao Jr., or Jr. NTR, is a beloved and celebrated actor in Telugu cinema. He is the grandson of the legendary actor and politician Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR) and has carved his own identity in the industry with his powerful performances. Jr. NTR made his debut as a child artist but gained widespread recognition with Student No. 1 (2001). Over the years, he has starred in numerous hit films, such as Temper, Janatha Garage, and RRR. NTR is known for his versatility, switching seamlessly between intense action, drama, and dance-heavy roles. His performances are known for their raw energy, particularly his electrifying dance moves, which have earned him immense popularity. His role in RRR (2023) brought him international acclaim, showcasing his acting skills on a global platform. Jr. NTR's down-to-earth personality and his connection with his fans have made him one of the most admired stars in the Telugu film industry. His immense popularity, both on and off-screen, has made him an enduring icon in Indian cinema.
